SUPREME COURT OF NIGERIA - LANDMARK CASES
=========================================

RECENT LANDMARK CASES (2023-2024)
=================================

1. NATIONAL INLAND WATERWAYS AUTHORITY v. LAGOS STATE GOVERNMENT (2024)
-----------------------------------------------------------------------
Date: January 5, 2024
Citation: SC.1/2024
Justices: 7-member panel led by CJN Kekere-Ekun

Facts:
- Dispute over jurisdiction and control of inland waterways
- Lagos State claimed authority over waterways within its territory
- Federal Government through NIWA claimed exclusive jurisdiction

Issues:
- Whether states have concurrent jurisdiction over inland waterways
- Extent of federal exclusive powers over maritime matters
- Revenue sharing from waterway activities

Held:
- Federal Government has exclusive jurisdiction over navigable waterways
- States retain authority over non-navigable internal waters
- Revenue sharing formula must reflect constitutional provisions

Significance:
- Clarified federal-state jurisdictional boundaries
- Important for maritime law and revenue allocation
- Precedent for future waterway disputes

2. FEDERAL GOVERNMENT v. 36 STATE GOVERNMENTS (Local Government Autonomy Case) (2024)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Date: July 11, 2024
Citation: SC.343/2024
Justices: Full court (7 justices)

Facts:
- Federal Government sued all 36 state governments
- Alleged unconstitutional control over local government funds
- States accused of undermining local government autonomy

Issues:
- Constitutional status of local governments
- Direct allocation of funds to local governments
- State interference in local government administration

Held:
- Local governments are constitutionally recognized third tier of government
- Federal allocation must go directly to local governments
- States cannot dissolve democratically elected local government councils
- Joint State-Local Government accounts are unconstitutional

Significance:
- Landmark decision on fiscal federalism
- Strengthened local government autonomy
- Major constitutional interpretation on governance structure

3. LOTTERY AND GAMING REGULATION CASE (2024)
--------------------------------------------
Date: October 14, 2024
Citation: SC.567/2024
Justices: 5-member panel

Facts:
- Multiple states claimed authority to regulate lottery operations
- Federal Government asserted exclusive jurisdiction
- Conflict over licensing and taxation of gaming activities

Issues:
- Whether lottery regulation falls under federal or state jurisdiction
- Taxation powers over gaming activities
- Licensing authority for gambling operations

Held:
- Lottery and gaming regulation is on concurrent legislative list
- Both federal and state governments can regulate within their spheres
- Federal law prevails in case of conflict
- States can impose additional regulations not inconsistent with federal law

Significance:
- Clarified gambling regulation framework
- Important for revenue generation
- Balanced federal-state regulatory powers

4. RIVERS STATE POLITICAL CRISIS CASE (2024)
--------------------------------------------
Date: Multiple hearings throughout 2024
Citation: SC.234/2024

Facts:
- Political crisis in Rivers State affecting governance
- Dispute over control of state assembly and executive
- Federal intervention in state fund allocation

Issues:
- Federal power to intervene in state political crises
- Constitutional requirements for state governance
- Protection of democratic institutions

Held:
- Federal Government can intervene to protect constitutional democracy
- State funds can be withheld pending resolution of constitutional crisis
- Democratic institutions must be protected from unconstitutional actions

Significance:
- Precedent for federal intervention in state crises
- Protection of democratic governance
- Balance between federal oversight and state autonomy

HISTORIC LANDMARK CASES
=======================

5. ATTORNEY GENERAL OF ONDO STATE v. ATTORNEY GENERAL OF THE FEDERATION (1982)
------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Citation: (1982) 3 NCLR 807

Facts:
- Dispute over revenue allocation formula
- Ondo State challenged federal retention of oil revenues
- Constitutional interpretation of resource control

Issues:
- Derivation principle in revenue allocation
- Federal versus state control of natural resources
- Constitutional provisions on revenue sharing

Held:
- Natural resources belong to the Federal Government
- Derivation principle applies but with federal oversight
- States entitled to fair share based on constitutional formula

Significance:
- Foundation case for resource control disputes
- Established federal supremacy over natural resources
- Still cited in modern revenue allocation cases

6. AWOLOWO v. SHAGARI (1979)
---------------------------
Citation: (1979) 6-9 SC 51

Facts:
- Presidential election petition
- Challenge to 1979 presidential election results
- Constitutional interpretation of election requirements

Issues:
- Requirements for valid presidential election
- Geographical spread requirement
- Burden of proof in election petitions

Held:
- Presidential candidate must satisfy constitutional requirements
- Geographical spread is mandatory for legitimacy
- High burden of proof required for election nullification

Significance:
- Landmark election law case
- Established precedent for presidential election disputes
- Constitutional interpretation of democratic requirements

7. MARWA v. NYAKO (2012)
-----------------------
Citation: (2012) 9 NWLR (Pt. 1304) 199

Facts:
- Gubernatorial election dispute in Adamawa State
- Challenge to election results and procedures
- Constitutional issues in state elections

Issues:
- Validity of gubernatorial elections
- Electoral procedures and constitutional compliance
- Jurisdiction of election petition tribunals

Held:
- Strict compliance with electoral laws required
- Constitutional procedures must be followed
- Election tribunals have exclusive jurisdiction over election disputes

Significance:
- Important for state election law
- Procedural requirements for valid elections
- Jurisdictional clarity for election disputes

8. UGOCHUKWU v. ARARAUME (2007)
------------------------------
Citation: (2007) 12 NWLR (Pt. 1048) 365

Facts:
- Senatorial election dispute
- Challenge to primary election and general election
- Party nomination procedures

Issues:
- Validity of party primary elections
- Constitutional requirements for candidacy
- Court jurisdiction over party internal affairs

Held:
- Valid party nomination is prerequisite for election
- Courts have limited jurisdiction over party primaries
- Constitutional requirements must be satisfied

Significance:
- Clarified party nomination procedures
- Limited court intervention in party affairs
- Important for electoral jurisprudence

9. WOMEN'S INHERITANCE RIGHTS CASES (2023-2024)
-----------------------------------------------
Multiple cases addressing customary law vs constitutional rights

Key Cases:
- Mojekwu v. Ejikeme (revisited)
- Various inheritance disputes across different states

Issues:
- Conflict between customary law and constitutional rights
- Gender equality in inheritance
- Modernization of traditional practices

Recent Holdings:
- Constitutional rights supersede discriminatory customary practices
- Women have equal inheritance rights regardless of customary law
- Traditional practices must conform to constitutional principles

Significance:
- Progressive interpretation of women's rights
- Modernization of customary law
- Gender equality advancement
